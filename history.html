Our History

Since its founding in 2007, Maki High School has grown from a small community institution into one of the most respected schools in the Kilimanjaro region.

Founding and Motto

Maki High School was established in 2007 in Rombo District, Kilimanjaro, Tanzania. The name “Maki” comes from the two villages Marangu and Kitowo. Guided by the motto “Education for Development”, the school began with only two classroom blocks, limited facilities, and five dedicated teachers led by the first headmaster, Mr. Ndesa. Despite these humble beginnings, Maki welcomed 150 pioneer students and set out to provide quality ordinary level education to the community.

Growth into a High School

Maki expanded its academic offerings by introducing Advanced Level classes. This important milestone transformed Maki from a secondary school into a recognized high school, raising its reputation across the Rombo District and beyond. The school became known not only for academic excellence but also for achievements in sports and extracurricular activities.

  • 3 students reached district level in football competitions.
  • 1 student competed at regional level in basketball.
  • Maki led in Chemistry at the ordinary level examinations.
  • Introduction of computers for student learning and digital literacy.
  • Launch of creative clubs such as the Art Club, giving students opportunities to explore talents.

Community and Legacy

Maki High School has always believed in shaping not only strong students but also responsible citizens. The school actively engages in scout activities and community service, encouraging learners to give back to society and develop teamwork, discipline, and compassion.

Through these initiatives, Maki fosters a spirit of service and leadership. Students are given opportunities to explore their talents, contribute to their communities, and prepare for future responsibilities. The school looks forward to creating good leaders who will drive development and inspire positive change in Tanzania and beyond.
